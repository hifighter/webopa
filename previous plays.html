VYAGRAHAROGAN

This a still from a stageplay titled VYAGRAHAROGAN performed at 'RANGMANCH' at SRIJAN-The cultural fest of IIT ISM Dhanbad, under the guidance of Mr. Nalini nihar nayak and two of the ex presidents of our club Mr. Biswaranjan naik and Mr. Deepak Thakur we were able to secure FIRST POSITION at the competition among all the participating teams and won a prize worth INR 15,000. The title 'Vyagraharohan' when translated stands 'Vyagraha' for 'bagh or tiger' and 'arohan' for 'riding'.The play beautifully depicted how materialism and false pride can consume a person's ability to think rationally and thereby leading to their downfall. The play revolves around a mighty and arrogant king Abhivantra whose boastful nature and falsified pride leads to his great loss. A tiger is captured by the kings-men and brought before the king. Instead of treating the matter cautiously, to the safety of his people, the king is rather careless and boastful and commands his younger son to ride the tiger away from the kingdom and loses his son henceforth for the sake of his pride and glory. The play ends with the king realising his mistake and giving a beautiful moral to implement in our lives.

INDEPENDENCE DAY:MIME Act 2K18

On the auspicious occasion of the 72nd Independence Day of our country, Pantomime got a chance to perform at the Independence Day celebrations held at the jam-packed BBA. Esteemed dignitaries, professors and students of the institute were present at the celebrations to increase its grandeur. Pantomime chose to perform a mime act and based their act on showcasing the challenging life of an Indian soldier. The Indian soldiers are the supreme protectors of your country who put their life and comfort at stake so that we can enjoy the coziness of the sweet cocoons of our home. Pantomime’s performance was an ode to these brave men. The mime act was very well scripted and systematically showcased the various hurdles that a soldier faces in the difficult path which he has chosen. It started from the very beginning of the soldier’s life and emphasized on social values like friendship, love and sacrifice. It showed how a child who is inspired and is determined to serve his nation leaves his family and loved ones to undergo vigorous training, undertakes a tough selection process and gets chosen for protecting the nation even at extreme conditions, always ready to sacrifice his life. The young and talented performers of the club played their assigned roles in a synchronized and regulated manner to produce a brilliant performance. All the gimmicks were made quickly and efficiently without any delays. Some scenes of the play moved the audience to tears and were later appraised a lot. The technical aspects of the play were also managed very efficiently by the club members and the proper lights and sound added to the essence of the play. Overall, the actors performed the play with full energy and exuberance. The audience rose to a standing ovation and praised team Pantomime for making it such a memorable one. The play left a strong message and an impact on the minds of the audience

INNOVISION MIME ACT

Odyssey: an epic journey.

On the opening day of this fest, the official dramatic club of NITR, Pantomime performed a mime act for the same. The gang was ready and assembled for the task. The club opted to show the entire human evolution and hence the innovation of science and technology. PANTOMIME INDUCTION 2018 NUKKAD

Performing nukkad is the best way to bring the social awareness. Nukkad is the way to represent a social issue in the best descriptive way. Pantomime performed nukkad to raise the issue of sex education, corruption, and drugs abuse under the guidance of the seniors and it never failed to show the enthusiasm and dedication even under the rain. Children falling in the age group 12_15 lack knowledge about sex because they haven’t been taught about it, which results in increased curiosity among them for the same. Pantomime showed the story of a girl who was much curious about it. Though her curiosity increased when her father denied to teach her about it. The second part of the nukkad raised the issue of corruption. Corruption implies perversion of morality, integrity, character without any regard to honour and justice. Pantomime showed the story of a job aspirant who suffered due to the influence of higher authority. Lastly pantomime showed the story of a student who was affected badly due the peer pressure for having drugs.

IIT KGP 2K19

“Kale kurte main jo aag hain poori duniya ye khaak hain….” Was the start anthem of our script of the street play named “ SACH KA SAMNA.” We never knew that out of random things, forming a team, formulating a solid script and executing the idea of good vs bad media and the most important of all taking that script to IIT Kharagpur and competing in their fest will fall in place. As we hear that a prize demands handwork and perseverance the practice days were a complete phase in which all the members had put their heart and soul to make the street play wear its best outfit. Our theme revolved around how bad media influences the crowd’s mind and the hoax scenario affects elections, thoughts etc. we had focused on a direct comparison of good media and bad media and the ugly truth about the same. And after a short but long seemed session of rehearsals and practices we were ready to see the actual players of street play business. Experiencing Springfest is a complete different horizon because it sets the mark for a cultural fest so high. But dodging the glamour we focussed on our street play and were in constant prepflow. And finally the performance day arrived and our anxiousness was at its peak and observing other strong teams the situation worsened. But we had to give our best and we put our sweat and blood in those 15 minutes and we came out of the play circle feeling relieved. Unfortunately we had to digest the fact that we could not win the competition but we left Kharagpur with a content heart and a lesson…
